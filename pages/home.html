Academics

Research Interests

Lately, I've been exploring two things.

  1. High-performance computing: the programming languages that support them, parallel computing and algorithms.
  2. Morpholigically Rich Languages: their computational complexity, and a phonetic algorithm for Tagalog. I belong to the group being advised by Nathaniel Oco.

In general, I enjoy discussing and researching topics in Theoretical Computer Science, especially when they intersect with real-world applications or other fields.

I'm especially interested in real-world puzzles that can be efficiently solved through creative applications of algorithms and data structures.

My previous work spanned the following topics:

  • Numeric algorithms and geometry
  • Computational complexity
  • Computational puzzles
